Saphala Platform — Pre-Launch Architecture Decision Audit

Context:
We currently have two separate Replit codebases:
1. Saphala Admin app
2. Saphala Self Prep / Student Frontend app

Both connect to the same Neon database.

Problem:
Because these are separate codebases, there is risk of future mismatch in:
- auth / email-mobile normalization
- duplicate checks
- taxonomy usage
- content visibility / publish logic
- test and analytics assumptions
- shared DB contract drift

Idea under consideration:
Move toward one shared codebase / monorepo structure while still keeping 2 separate apps and 2 separate login experiences:
- admin app remains separate UI/app
- student app remains separate UI/app
- but shared DB logic / validation / types / schema / helpers are centralized

Important:
The platform needs to launch in 2 days.
So I do NOT want an ideal theoretical answer only.
I want a realistic, code-grounded, launch-safe recommendation.

Task:
Please do a pure architecture audit and recommendation.

Questions to answer:

1. Is merging the two Replit codebases into one shared monorepo with two apps a better long-term solution for this platform?
2. Is it safe to attempt before launch in 2 days?
3. What are the risks of doing it now?
4. What are the risks of NOT doing it now?
5. What is the best launch-safe approach?

Please compare these options:

Option A — Keep both codebases separate for launch
- but lock a strict shared DB contract and QA checklist

Option B — Partial consolidation before launch
- share only critical logic such as schema/auth/validation/helpers
- keep apps otherwise separate

Option C — Full monorepo consolidation before launch
- apps/admin
- apps/student
- shared packages

For each option, evaluate:
- launch risk
- implementation effort
- regression risk

- deployment risk
- future maintainability
- Replit suitability
- whether it can be completed safely in time
